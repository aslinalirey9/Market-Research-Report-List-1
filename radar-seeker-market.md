<p><p>In the radar seeker market, two primary types are identified: initiative and passive. Initiative seekers actively emit signals and process the reflected waves, allowing for targeted tracking and engagement of objects, typically seen in advanced missile systems. Conversely, passive seekers do not emit signals but rely on detecting emissions from targets, making them stealthier and harder to detect. This distinction influences applications in military and defense strategies, with each type offering unique advantages in target acquisition and operational effectiveness.</p></p>
