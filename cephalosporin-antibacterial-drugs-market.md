<p><p>The Cephalosporin antibacterial drugs market is categorized into four generations, each with distinct characteristics. First-generation cephalosporins primarily target gram-positive bacteria and are often used for skin infections. Second-generation cephalosporins have enhanced activity against gram-negative bacteria, making them suitable for respiratory infections. Third-generation cephalosporins offer broad-spectrum coverage and are effective for severe infections. Fourth-generation cephalosporins provide extended gram-negative coverage and are used in nosocomial infections, reflecting an ongoing evolution to combat resistant strains in clinical settings.</p></p>
