<p><p>The biochip products market comprises two main types: active chips and passive chips. Active chips are equipped with integrated circuits that perform specific functions, such as signal processing and data analysis, making them suitable for dynamic applications like diagnostics and research. In contrast, passive chips lack internal circuitry and rely on external systems for functionality, often serving as sensors or arrays for molecular analysis. Both types play crucial roles in personalized medicine, drug discovery, and disease detection.</p></p>
