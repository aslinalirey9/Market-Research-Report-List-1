<p><p>Thermopile sensors are devices that convert thermal energy into electrical voltage, allowing for non-contact temperature measurement. They consist of multiple thermocouples connected in series or parallel, which measure temperature differences and generate a voltage output proportional to the detected infrared radiation. <p><p>The thermopile sensor market is segmented into thermopile infrared sensors and thermopile laser sensors. Thermopile infrared sensors detect temperature by measuring infrared radiation, commonly used in non-contact temperature measurements and HVAC systems. In contrast, thermopile laser sensors utilize laser light to enhance measurement accuracy and are often employed in industrial applications, medical devices, and scientific research. Both types capitalize on thermoelectric principles to convert thermal energy into electrical signals, offering reliable performance across diverse sectors.</p></p>
